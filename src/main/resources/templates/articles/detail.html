Is Apple performing Monopoly?

By Yewon Kim
May 5, 2024 5 minutes to read

Founded in 1976, Apple Inc. is a leader in the consumer electronics industry, known for its iconic products such as the iPhone and Macintosh computers. Renowned for its elegant design and cutting-edge technology, Apple sets the standard for innovation. However, the company has recently been sued by the U.S Department of Justice.

The DoJ sued Apple for monopolizing the smartphone market, posing a significant challenge for the country's launch of its brand-new product. The lawsuit alleges that Apple has unlawfully maintained its monopoly power on the grounds against the tech giant Apple. Finally, there is the exclusivity of AppStore. Apple introduced AppPlay as a security measure for users and did not allow third-party applications to access the chip that enables the iPhone to make mobile payments. Consequently, users were forced to stick with iPhones, making it harder for consumers to switch to alternative brands such as Android rivals, such as smart watches, less useful than the Apple Watch. Apple was not the first to produce smartwatches. However, other companies that entered the smartwatch market earlier were rendered irrelevant due to Apple's strategy of making the Apple Watch with the iPhone. Lastly, the iPhone only allows users to download apps from Apple's proprietary App Store, a practice that resembles a monopoly.

Analysts predict that Apple is preparing a counter-attack in AI, and other analysts suggest that Apple's weakening presence in the Chinese market could quickly grow in the AI market. Apple faces a significant challenge in the generation of AI. Not only should the Department of Justice think carefully about this situation, but smartphone users should also consider whether Apple is the right company for their AI future.
